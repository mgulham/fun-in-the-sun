UV Index

The Ultraviolet (UV) Index predicts the ultraviolet radiation levels on a 1-11+ scale. The UV Index provides a daily forecast of the expected intensity of UV radiation from the sun.

Clothing Recommendations

Hot climates and a high UV index can cause skin conditions as well as many other health concerns. This is why it is important to keep up to date with what outfits are appropriate for the current weather.

Tightly woven clothing that cover the skin will offer the best protection.

UV rays can damage your eyesight and cause vision issues like cataracs. It is also imperative to wear sunglasses that block 100% of UV rays.

Another great measure is to wear a hat. Your face has very sensitive skin that can be vulnerable to burning. A hat can also help protect your eyes when you don't have glasses.

Health Recommendations

There are 2 types of ultraviolet radiation, UVA and UVB. Both UV radiations damage the skin, age it prematurely, and increase your risk of skin cancer. One of the most important measures in protecting yourself from UV radiation is wearing the proper sunscreen. A sunscreen with at least SPF 15 is great, but SPF 30 is recommended.
